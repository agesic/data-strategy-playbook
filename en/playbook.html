Playbook For Self-Assessment And Preparation Of Data Strategies In The Public Sector In Latin America And The Caribbean

Data Management Maturity in Organizations

To achieve a sustainable and successful Data Strategy over time, it is necessary for the Organization to have a minimum degree of digital and data maturity in order to advance in its design.

Before starting the process of building a Data Strategy, it is important to evaluate the maturity of the organization in this area. This will provide the organization with an initial diagnosis of the strengths and weaknesses, which will facilitate the creation of a roadmap that allows prioritizing the necessary actions to achieve the desired data maturity.

Null Low Medium High
There is a lack of information or actions reflecting maturity in the government's digital and data infrastructure. Initial actions in policy, leadership, and planning are recommended to begin building the foundation of a Data Strategy. The actions and initiatives are in an early stage of development. It is recommended to consolidate and institutionalize policies, organizational structures, objectives, and specific actions prior to designing a Data Strategy.

The government has sufficient actions and initiatives for the development of a sustainable Data Strategy.

It is recommended to monitor the initiatives and identify areas for improvement, as well as maintain constant engagement with the data user community to scale data projects that generate social and economic value.

 The government has ideal actions and initiatives for the development of a scalable and sustainable Data Strategy.

Self-assessment of the organization's degree of digital data maturity

1. Leadership

It refers to the existence of a high-level political commitment and a data strategy as an instrument that crosscuts all government institutions.

1.1 Is there a high-level political commitment to build a comprehensive data strategy?
Condition Null Low Medium High
1.1 Is there a high-level political commitment to build a comprehensive data strategy? Not Available Public statements by the Chief Executive or at Ministerial level Commitment in the Development Plan or an international public commitment Presidential Decree or Bill/Law
Recommendations for raising the level of maturity to MEDIUM Best Practices
  1. Manifest the government's interest in initiating a data strategy through a public statement by the Chief Executive or a Minister or high-ranking official. The commitment can be expressed in terms of: objective in the Development Plan, work plans, adherence or signing of an international mechanism (eg Open Government Partnership / International Open Data Charter).
  2. Form a multi-stakeholder working group to promote the drafting and publication by the Executive of a regulatory instrument that justifies and institutionalizes a data strategy. This can materialize through a proposal or bill, mandates or decrees.

1.2 Is there a person with a defined role who leads the comprehensive data strategy and who has cross-cutting influence in government institutions?
Condition Null Low Medium High
1.2 Is there a person with a defined role who leads the comprehensive data strategy and who has cross-cutting influence in government institutions? Not Available There is a person or team that leads initiatives related to data, however, they do not have the leadership or cross-cutting powers or empowerment in terms of defining initiatives, strategy or budget. There is a person who is considered the Data Leader with some transversal attributions but without a defined role in the government's organizational structure. There is the role of National Data Leader with an office or team specifically dedicated to the implementation of the data strategy, with defined attributions and budget.
Recommendations for raising the level of maturity to MEDIUM Best Practices
  1. Identify the right area or person to lead initiatives related to the use of data with the potential to convene and link other government areas. These people commonly highlight areas of innovation, digital government, statistics, transparency, economic development, science and technology, among others.
  2. Define the specific responsibilities of a Data Leader who has the political support of the Executive and who can influence other government areas across the board.
  3. Institutionalize the role of the Data Leader in the organizational structure with attributions, budget and an advisory team to be able to coordinate the necessary government areas and make strategic decisions. This institutionalization can be given through an official appointment, decree or organizational restructuring.

1.3 Is there an institutionalized data governance model that integrates all types of data, and legal, technological, financial elements, etc., with which it is related?
Condition Null Low Medium High
1.3 Is there an institutionalized data governance model that integrates all types of data, and legal, technological, financial elements, etc., with which it is related? Not Available There are clear roles, responsibilities and legal and infrastructure elements that should be coordinated to form a governance model, however, there is no institutionalized model. The model is under development. Some elements (legal, political, financial, organizational and technological) already operate in a coordinated manner. There is a Data Governance Model with the responsibilities, processes and sufficient elements to coordinate a comprehensive data policy in a sustainable manner.
Recommendations for raising the level of maturity to MEDIUM Best Practices
  1. Carry out a mapping of elements (legal, technological, human resources, etc.) that require linkage for efficient data management and that promotes its use.
  2. Prepare a document that establishes the roles, responsibilities, and processes for data management by the institutions that considers a holistic vision with the elements mentioned above.
  3. Institutionalize said document through a decree, mandate, or bill.
